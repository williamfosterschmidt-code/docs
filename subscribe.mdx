*Life on a Fault Line* is a free newsletter. Subscribing means new essays arrive directly in your inbox — no social feed to scroll, no algorithm deciding whether you see them, no cost. Just writing, when it's ready.

## What you get

When you subscribe, you join a small reader community receiving personal essays from Will Foster-Schmidt. These are long-form pieces — the kind that take time to read and are written to stay with you. They arrive without a fixed schedule, because good essays don't run on a content calendar.

<Note>
  This is not a daily newsletter. Essays are published when they're finished — which means you won't be flooded with email, and what arrives is worth opening.
</Note>

## How to subscribe

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the newsletter page">
    Visit [lifeonafaultline.substack.com](https://lifeonafaultline.substack.com/subscribe) in your browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your email address">
    Type your email into the subscribe field. Substack handles everything securely — your address is never sold or shared.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your subscription">
    Check your inbox for a confirmation email from Substack and click the link inside. This prevents unwanted subscriptions and ensures delivery.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive new essays when published">
    That's it. Each time a new essay goes out, it lands in your inbox. You can also read everything on the Substack archive at any time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to expect

The essays in *Life on a Fault Line* cover grief, the end of a marriage, the strange comfort of music, and the ongoing, unglamorous work of becoming a fuller version of yourself. They are personal — drawn from real experience — and written to be honest rather than tidy.

Will has been publishing for about five months. There is already a body of work in the archive, and more is on the way.
